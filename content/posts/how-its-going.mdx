Currently running on coffee, C++, research papers, and an unreasonable amount of curiosity.

I'm currently pursuing my M.Tech in Computer Science and working as a Student Intern at Nokia, where I spend most of my time building software, debugging things that were definitely working yesterday, and discovering that every "quick fix" has the potential to become a full evening activity.

Somewhere between writing my first C++ program and building telecom software, I realized that what I enjoy most isn't a specific language or framework—it's the process of turning an idea into something that actually works. That's what keeps me coming back.

These days I'm working on telecom software, automation frameworks, and research in edge-cloud computing. Which sounds very professional until you see the number of browser tabs, terminal windows, and half-finished side projects currently fighting for my attention.

I like building things that solve real problems. Sometimes that's a research project, sometimes it's enterprise software, and sometimes it's an idea that randomly appears at midnight and refuses to leave me alone until I've built it.

This portfolio is basically a reflection of that mindset.

I'm actively learning, building, experimenting, and pushing myself to create better things than I did yesterday.

I'm actively building it,
breaking it,
improving it,
and occasionally questioning every design decision I've made.

So if something looks unfinished, don't worry.

That's not a bug.

It's a future feature 🚀
